# cognitive-load-fatigue-sleep-analysis
Synthetic data exploration of cognitive load, fatigue, and sleep patterns with research-aligned

## Key Insight
The scatter plot revealed an unexpected pattern: fatigue did not increase as cognitive load increased. Instead, the trendline showed a weak negative relationship. This aligns with published research indicating that momentary cognitive load and subjective fatigue often do not correlate strongly.
This outcome highlights the importance of validating assumptions against actual data, even when working with synthetic datasets.

## What I Learned
- Visual expectations do not always match the data.
- Real research shows cognitive load and fatigue often have weak or inconsistent relationships.
- Sleep varies independently and is not a reliable predictor of either variable.
- Trendlines can reveal unexpected patterns worth exploring.
- Synthetic data must still be interpreted with scientific integrity.

- ## Limitations
- The dataset is synthetic and not collected from real participants.
- Fatigue is influenced by duration, mood, and context, which are not represented here.
- Cognitive load is treated as a momentary value rather than a time-based measure.
- Sleep duration does not capture sleep quality or circadian factors.
